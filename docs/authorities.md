# The Ben Franklin Society
## Of [County Name]

### Foundational Document on Authority
*Article I of the Bylaws*

---

### Preamble

All authority within the Ben Franklin Society of [County Name] originates with and belongs to the community in its entirety. No office, body, or individual possesses authority by right of their own. Authority is held by the community, delegated by the community for practical purposes, and may be recalled by the community at any time. This document establishes the structure by which that authority is organized and exercised.

---

### Article I — The Community

**§1. Original Authority.** The community, comprising all members of the Ben Franklin Society in good standing, is the original and final source of all authority within the Society. This authority is inherent and inalienable. No act of delegation diminishes it.

**§2. Delegation.** The community delegates the practical exercise of its authority to the Assembly and, through the Assembly, to the Colleges and their Committees. Such delegation is made for practical purposes only and does not transfer ownership of authority.

**§3. Override and Recall.** The community retains the right to override, amend, or revoke any decision, ordinance, or action of the Assembly, any College, or any Committee. The mechanisms for community override shall be established in the standing rules of the Society. *(Placeholder: petition threshold and referendum process to be defined.)*

**§4. Membership.** Criteria for membership in the Society, and thus participation in community authority, shall be established in the standing rules. *(Placeholder: eligibility, admission, and standing criteria to be defined.)*

### Article II — The Assembly

**§5. Establishment.** The community delegates its primary legislative authority to a body known as the Assembly. The Assembly acts on behalf of the community and at the community's pleasure.

**§6. Composition.** The Assembly shall consist of [X]% of the eligible adult membership of the Society, drawn by sortition. *(Placeholder: minimum and maximum assembly size to be defined in standing rules.)*

**§7. Sortition.** Members of the Assembly are selected by lot from the pool of all eligible adult members of the Society. Service on the Assembly is a civic obligation of membership. *(Placeholder: grounds for declining service to be defined in standing rules.)*

**§8. Term.** Assembly members shall serve terms of one year. No member shall serve consecutive terms without an intervening period of at least one year. *(Placeholder: transition and overlap provisions to be defined.)*

**§9. Legislative Authority.** The Assembly holds the primary legislative authority of the Society, acting within bounds set by the community. The Assembly may establish, amend, or revoke ordinances of general application. The Assembly may delegate legislative authority to Committees within defined domains.

**§10. Override Authority.** The Assembly may at any time override, amend, or revoke any ordinance or decision of any Committee. The Assembly may not override a decision of the community.

### Article III — The Colleges

**§11. Nature of the Colleges.** A College is a self-governing body of peers bound by common purpose, mutual obligation, and shared standards within a defined domain of community life. The word *College* is used in its original sense — *collegium* — a body of people mutually bound in delegation. Colleges are not instruments of hierarchy over the community but institutions of competence within it.

**§12. Establishment.** Colleges are established by the Assembly. The Assembly shall define the domain of each College and the standards of competence required for membership. Colleges may be dissolved or reorganized by the Assembly with the concurrence of the community.

**§13. Membership.** Membership in a College is open to any Society member who meets the standards of competence established by the Assembly for that College. Admission is determined by the College through peer evaluation. A member may belong to more than one College.

**§14. Standards and Credentialing.** Each College is responsible for maintaining the standards of competence within its domain, evaluating candidates for membership, and developing the knowledge and practice of its field. The Assembly establishes the framework of standards; the College administers them.

**§15. Institutional Life.** A College has ongoing institutional existence independent of its Committee functions. College members are expected to confer, develop shared knowledge, and maintain the standards of their domain continuously, not only when Committee service is required.

### Article IV — Committees

**§17. Nature of Committees.** A Committee is the governance-active body drawn from a College. Committees exercise delegated legislative authority within their domain on behalf of the Assembly and ultimately the community. Committee members are drawn by sortition from the membership of their College.

**§18. Permanent Committees.** Each established College shall maintain a permanent Committee responsible for ongoing governance within its domain. Permanent Committees operate continuously and are responsible for the day-to-day and week-to-week governance of their domain.

**§19. Ad Hoc Committees.** The Assembly or a permanent Committee may convene an ad hoc Committee from the relevant College to address a specific question or project. Ad hoc Committees are dissolved upon completion of their designated purpose.

**§20. Composition and Sortition.** Committee members are selected by lot from the eligible membership of their College. *(Placeholder: Committee size, term length, and rotation provisions to be defined in standing rules.)*

**§21. Ordinance Authority.** Committees may draft, enact, amend, and revoke ordinances within their domain by delegation from the Assembly. Ordinances enacted by a Committee have the force of law within the Society unless and until overridden by the Assembly or the community. Committees shall act within the bounds established by the Assembly.

**§22. Relationship to the College.** Committee members are expected to consult with the broader membership of their College in the exercise of their authority. The College is a living resource of knowledge and judgment; the Committee is its governance expression at any given time.

**§23. Override.** The Assembly may override, amend, or revoke any Committee ordinance or decision at any time. The community may override any Committee ordinance or decision at any time.

---

### Article V — The Chain of Authority

**§24. Structure.** Authority within the Society flows as follows: from the Community to the Assembly by delegation; from the Assembly to the Committees by delegation; from the Committees to practical governance within their domains. At each level, authority is held in trust for the level above.

**§25. Accountability.** Each body in the chain of authority is accountable to the body above it and ultimately to the community. No body may act beyond the bounds of its delegated authority. Acts in excess of delegated authority are void.

**§26. Supremacy of the Community.** In all things and at all times, the authority of the community is supreme. The Assembly, the Colleges, and the Committees exist to serve the community, not to govern it against its will. When the community speaks, all other bodies must yield.
