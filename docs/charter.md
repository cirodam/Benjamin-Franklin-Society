# The Ben Franklin Society
## Charter — First Draft

### Preamble

There are moments in history that test the character of a nation — that demand more than ordinary times prepare anyone for. We believe we are living in one. We do not take that lightly. We resolve to meet it together.

During this nation's founding — another such moment — Benjamin Franklin didn't wait. He built what his community needed with his own hands and his neighbors'. It is in his spirit that we found the Ben Franklin Society of Franklin County, Georgia. These are the principles we build by.

---

### I. Human Dignity

We hold that human dignity is unconditional. It cannot be earned or lost, granted or revoked. You have it because you are human — wherever you were born, whatever you have done, whoever you are. This is the foundation everything else rests on.

### II. Self-Governance

We govern ourselves through genuine deliberation. No authority is legitimate here that does not flow from the genuine consent of the people subject to it. We trust ordinary people to make good decisions — not ideology, not party, not wealth.

### III. Honest Inquiry

We believe in honest inquiry. The world is what it is regardless of what we wish it to be. We commit to following evidence wherever it leads.

### IV. Mutual Obligation

We accept collective responsibility for every member's food, care, and security. Mutual obligation, not charity.

### V. Economic Sovereignty

Economic sovereignty belongs to the people who live and work in a community. We reject systematic exploitation in all its forms.

### VI. Building Our Own

We build our own institutions rather than waiting for failing ones. We are practitioners first — we measure success not by what we believe but by what our community can do.

### VII. Stewardship

We are stewards of the natural world. We treat land, water, and living systems as held in trust for those who come after us.

### VIII. Common Purpose

We protect our capacity to think and decide for ourselves. We build community life that restores genuine connection and common purpose.

### IX. Accountability

We hold ourselves to genuine accountability — to each other, to our principles, and to all people.
